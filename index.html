  • Graphic Design

    History's Role for the Future

    These compositions take place during the early Renaissance period, while the pictures on the wall portray what will come of the future from that time period, specifically the late 1800s and early 1900s.

    The compositions were created to contrast the effects that knowledge has on society through time. The top image shows history being studied and a good future on the wall. The bottom image displays history being ignored and a threatening future on the wall.

    Created in Photoshop, they are composed of dozens of separate layers of content, including many lighting adjustments. The photos in the background were provided by the Library of Congress. The subject's face was created in Daz 3D, while the hair was painted directly in Photoshop. All other elements were created from photos I personally shot.

    Nature Rules

    This is a personal recreation of a composition titled, Nature Rules, which was originally created by the instructor of my college Photo Illustration instructor, Bret Malley. He supplied the artwork's source photography, which consisted of a basic photo of a city, a field, a mountain and a sky. I then completed the remaining work with Photoshop.

    A guide to follow was given, however I did quite a bit of work outside of the guide to enhance the shading, lighting and color.
